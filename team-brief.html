SafeAhead

See what's happened. Know what's coming. Protect your home.

One-line pitch: "Enter your home address. See what's actually happened there — floods, storms, fires, break-ins. Know what to do about it."

The Problem

When someone buys or owns a home, they have no easy way to see what has actually happened at or near that address. The insurer knows — they've been paying claims there for decades — but the customer is buying blind. They find out their area floods when their living room is underwater.

HMW: "How might we give homeowners transparent, data-backed risk information about their property so they can protect themselves before something goes wrong?"

Customer Pain Points

1. Buying blind — You buy a house, you don't know it floods every 3 years. The insurer knows (they priced your premium on it) but you never see the data.
2. No context for premiums — Premium goes up 30%. You don't know why. Is it your area? Your roof? A claim 5 years ago? No explanation, just a bigger bill.
3. Risks are invisible until they hit — You don't know your suburb has had 47 hail claims in the last 2 years until your car is dented. The pattern was there — nobody showed you.
4. Prevention is guesswork — Should you clear gutters? Get a roof inspection? Install a sump pump? Without knowing what goes wrong at properties like yours, you're guessing.
5. Data exists but is locked away — Flood maps, bushfire ratings, claims density, weather history — it all exists. None of it is presented to the customer in a useful way.

Root cause: Risk data is used to price policies (insurer benefit) but never shared with the customer to help them act (customer benefit).

Core Insight

The data already exists. Flood records, storm damage history, bushfire zones, claims patterns — all public or insurer-held. The problem isn't generating new data. It's that nobody puts it in front of the homeowner in a clear, address-specific, actionable way.

The Solution

A data transparency tool for home insurance. Enter your address, see your risk profile based on real historical data.

What you get:

  1. What has happened here — flood events, storm damage, bushfire proximity, break-ins, hail, subsidence. From public records (BOM, SES, RFS, councils, ICA, police stats).
  2. How often — frequency and recency. "Flooded 3 times in 10 years" is different from "once, 18 years ago."
  3. What that means for your home — plain-language summary. "Your area has high storm exposure. Most common damage: roof/gutter from hail."
  4. What you can do — specific prevention steps tied to what actually goes wrong here. Not generic tips.

What this is NOT:

Not a chatbot. Not "AI-powered" marketing. Not personalised profiling. Not a policy comparison tool. Not real-time alerts.
It's a data transparency tool. Look up your address. See what's happened. Know what to watch for.

MVP Features (for demo)

  1. Address input — enter an address, get a risk profile
  2. Incident timeline — visual history of events in the area (5–20 years)
  3. Risk breakdown — categorised: flood, storm, fire, theft, subsidence. With frequency data.
  4. Action list — top 3 things to protect your property, backed by what actually happens here

Stretch Features

  • Compare two addresses (useful when house hunting)
  • Seasonal patterns ("Storms peak here in November")
  • Policy gap highlighting based on your actual local risks
  • Neighbourhood heatmap view

Target Persona

Sarah, 28. First-time homeowner in Penrith (Western Sydney). NRMA home + contents insurance. Doesn't know the area floods. Doesn't know what to maintain. Premium seems expensive but she doesn't know why.

  • Transparency builds trust → customer retention
